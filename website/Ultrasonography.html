Ultrasonography


Definition :

Ultrasonography, also known as ultrasound imaging or diagnostic medical sonography, is a medical imaging
technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create visual images of the inside of the body.
It is a non-invasive and painless procedure that helps in diagnosing and monitoring various medical conditions.


Advantages :

Ultrasonography has several advantages. It does not involve the use of ionizing radiation, making it a safer option compared to techniques such as X-rays or CT scans. It is widely used in obstetrics and gynecology to monitor fetal development during pregnancy and detect any abnormalities. It is also commonly used to examine abdominal organs, such as the liver, gallbladder, kidneys, and pancreas, as well as to evaluate the heart, blood vessels, thyroid gland, and musculoskeletal system


Limitations :

It's important to note that while ultrasound is a valuable diagnostic tool, it does have limitations. It may not provide detailed images of structures that are located deep within the body or are obscured by bone or gas. In such cases, other imaging techniques like CT scans or MRI may be necessary. The interpretation of ultrasound images requires expertise and is typically performed by trained healthcare professionals, such as radiologists or sonographers.
